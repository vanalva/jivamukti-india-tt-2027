Land of action

There is something undeniably sacred about the journey to India, the land where the teachings of yoga were first heard as cosmic music by saints and sages, where people have been practicing those teachings for thousands of years without ceasing.

Let yourself be inspired and activated by the special magic of this journey, whether it be your first or you’ve been there many times before.

The Five Tenets of Jivamukti Yoga

India's sacred landscape is the perfect environment to deepen your understanding of the five core principles that form the foundation of Jivamukti Yoga.

Ahimsa

अहिंसा — Non-Violence

Indian cuisine naturally lends itself to a sattvic vegan diet, encouraging lightness and spirituality through compassionate eating. The animals living around the village in the safe sanctuary will teach us the power of compassion and unconditional love.

Nada

नाद — Sound

Kirtan is a way of life at Govardhan Ecovillage. The sounds of nature, devotion and kindness will help you develop the ability of listening and open your heart to the music of the universe.

Bhakti

भक्ति — Devotion

Temples and ashram community inspire the heart and mind. Devotion is practiced everywhere in the village — everyone is inspired to serve others and cultivate a life of pure dedication.

Dhyana

ध्यान — Meditation

Remove distractions of daily life so meditation practice becomes effortless. Practiced daily, meditation will become a regular ritual at the start and end of each study day, anchoring you in presence.

Shastra

शास्त्र — Scripture

Be surrounded by individuals who know the holy scriptures inside and out, and learn to use them as a guidepost for everyday life — a living connection to the ancient wisdom tradition of yoga.

About Bhakti, Radhanath Swami,
and the Journey Home

Bhakti is the yoga of devotion, which takes the energetic science of yoga from practice into an experience of transcendental joy. It is perhaps one of the most magical aspects of Jivamukti Yoga, expressed through chanting and spiritual storytelling.

The Jivamukti Method's sincere connection to traditional lineages and spiritual communities in India includes our association with Govardhan Ecovillage, the ashram home of Radhanath Swami.

The Journey Home is Radhanath Swami's inspiring story in which a search for God and Guru led the young American teen from Europe to India on foot, where he faced challenges, met treasured friends, and ultimately created the very place where you will travel for your own profound transformation.

Prerequisites

Preparing for the JYTT is a journey in itself, between reading modern and classic yoga texts, writing spiritual commentaries, practicing and memorizing two different 90 minute Jivamukti classes, and getting familiar with anatomy concepts. Don't miss the opportunity to be well prepared. As the training is very intense schedule wise and full of profound content, the more time you spend preparing the more likely it will be you can remain grounded during the experience.

Please ensure you have the required visa and immigration documents before traveling to India. Check with your local Indian embassy or consulate for the most current requirements. Visa processing times vary by country — we recommend applying well in advance of the program start date.
