Let yourself be inspired and activated by the special magic of this journey, whether it be your first or you’ve been there many times before.

About Bhakti, Radhanath Swami,
and the Journey Home

Bhakti

Bhakti is the yoga of devotion, which takes the energetic science of yoga from practice into an experience of transcendental joy. It is perhaps one of the most magical aspects of Jivamukti Yoga, expressed through chanting and spiritual storytelling.

Radhanath Swami

The Jivamukti Method's sincere connection to traditional lineages and spiritual communities in India includes our association with Govardhan Ecovillage, the ashram home of Radhanath Swami.

The Journey Home

The Journey Home is Radhanath Swami's inspiring story in which a search for God and Guru led the young American teen from Europe to India on foot, where he faced challenges, met treasured friends, and ultimately created the very place where you will travel for your own profound transformation.

Prerequisites

Preparing for the JYTT is a journey in itself, between reading modern and classic yoga texts, writing spiritual commentaries, practicing and memorizing two different 90 minute Jivamukti classes, and getting familiar with anatomy concepts. Don't miss the opportunity to be well prepared. As the training is very intense schedule wise and full of profound content, the more time you spend preparing the more likely it will be you can remain grounded during the experience. Please be sure to refer to the expanded instructions in the registration, also published here.

Arrival, Departure & Transfers

Arrive to Mumbai via Chhatrapati Shivaji International Airport by very latest morning of Saturday, February 6th, 2027. It is a nice option to book your flight to arrive a couple of days early to India to adjust to timezone, climate, etc. You can coordinate to meet with other JYTT students that are also arriving early via our dedicated JYTT Slack Channel. Upon arrival, you can travel directly to Govardhan Ecovillage or stay in Mumbai.

Saturday, February 6th is the day you should arrive to Govardhan Ecovillage, which is 2-3 hours drive from Mumbai. Please plan your arrival so you will arrive no later than 4pm to check in.

Depart from Govardhan Ecovillage: Anytime on Friday, March 5th, 2027. It is advisable for you to book your departing flights in the evening March 5th at the earliest, as the journey from GEV to Mumbai is about 3 hrs but could be longer due to traffic.

More Arrival Info

For those in need of transport upon arrival, it is strongly suggested that you use your hotel shuttle taxi service if available. Most hotels offer their own airport shuttle, please utilize their service and book in advance. Arrival can be daunting so make it easy and safe for yourself.

For those staying elsewhere or arriving early, please see the link below regarding transportation options to Mumbai city center or elsewhere. http://www.mumbaiairport.com/transportation.php

A bus will depart from Mumbai Airport's International Terminal (Terminal 2) on Saturday, Feb 6th:
The bus pick-up/drop-off area and parking zones are located at Level 1, Ground Transportation (P4). To get there, take the escalators behind Burger King.
Please ensure you are at the Bus Terminal by 9:30 AM for check-in and boarding.
Cost: 20-25€ per person, non-refundable
Booking: Please reserve your seat via Regpack by selecting the product "Bus Ride GEV" under the Tuition/Accommodation section.
Payment: Can be made directly through Regpack or via bank transfer (details available in the Regpack checkout form).

As the training officially begins on February 6th, we recommend arriving at GEV on this date.

If you plan to arrive earlier or extend your stay post-JYTT, please coordinate directly with GEV for accommodations via fom@ecovillage.org.in (please note: they may take a few days to respond).
During the Jivamukti Teacher Training the buffet will be gourmet vegan - please note this type of special catering will not be available if you extend your stay at GEV, the accommodation rates will reflect that.
